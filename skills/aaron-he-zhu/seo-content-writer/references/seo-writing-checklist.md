# SEO Writing Checklist and Content Template

---

## On-Page SEO Checklist

**Keyword Placement**:
- [ ] Primary keyword in title, H1, first 100 words, at least one H2, conclusion, meta description
- [ ] Secondary keywords in H2s/H3s
- [ ] Related terms throughout body

**Content Quality**:
- [ ] Comprehensive coverage of topic
- [ ] Original insights or data
- [ ] Actionable takeaways
- [ ] Examples and illustrations
- [ ] Expert quotes or citations (E-E-A-T)

**Readability**:
- [ ] Paragraphs of 3-5 sentences
- [ ] Varied sentence length
- [ ] Bullet points and lists
- [ ] Bold key phrases
- [ ] Table of contents for long content

**Technical**:
- [ ] Internal links to relevant pages (2-5)
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources (2-3)
- [ ] Image alt text with keywords
- [ ] URL slug includes keyword

---

## Content Writing Template

```markdown
# [H1 with Primary Keyword]

[Hook sentence] [Problem statement] [Promise — what reader will learn] [Primary keyword naturally]
[Brief overview — can be bullet points]

## [H2 - First Main Section with Secondary Keyword]
[Introduction 1-2 sentences]
[Main content with valuable information]
[Examples, data, or evidence]

### [H3 - Sub-section if needed]
[Detailed content]
- Point 1
- Point 2
- Point 3

## [H2 - Second Main Section]
[Content...]
> **Pro Tip**: [Highlighted tip or key insight]

| Column 1 | Column 2 | Column 3 |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Data | Data | Data |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### [Question from PAA or common query]?
[Direct, concise answer in 40-60 words for featured snippet opportunity]

## Conclusion
[Summary of key points — include primary keyword]
[Final thought or insight]
[Clear call-to-action]
```

---

## Featured Snippet Optimization

- **Definition Snippets**: "[Term] is [clear, concise definition in 40-60 words]"
- **List Snippets**: Clear numbered or bulleted lists under H2s
- **Table Snippets**: Comparison tables with clear headers
- **How-To Snippets**: Number each step: "Step 1:", "Step 2:", etc.

**Meta Description**: Discover proven email marketing best practices that help small businesses increase open rates, boost engagement, and drive sales. Start improving your campaigns today. (158 chars)

Want to know the secret behind small businesses that consistently turn subscribers into loyal customers? It's strategic email marketing.

Email marketing delivers an average ROI of $42 for every $1 spent. In this guide, you'll learn:
- How to build a quality email list that converts
- Proven strategies to increase open and click rates
- Advanced personalization techniques
- Common mistakes that kill email performance

## Why Email Marketing Matters for Small Businesses

**Key statistics**:
- 81% of SMBs rely on email as primary acquisition channel
- Email subscribers are 3x more likely to share content on social media
- Personalized emails generate 6x higher transaction rates

## Building a High-Quality Email List

### Use Strategic Opt-in Incentives
**Lead magnets that convert**: industry-specific templates, exclusive discounts, free tools, educational email courses.

> **Pro Tip**: The best lead magnets solve a specific, immediate problem for your target audience.

### Implement Double Opt-in

| Single Opt-in | Double Opt-in |
|---------------|---------------|
| More subscribers | Fewer subscribers |
| Higher bounce rates | Lower bounce rates |
| Lower engagement | Higher engagement |
| Spam risk | Better deliverability |

## Crafting Emails That Get Opened

**Subject line best practices**:
1. Keep under 50 characters
2. Create urgency without being spammy
3. Use numbers and specifics
4. Personalize when possible
5. A/B test consistently

**Preview text**: Expand on subject line, don't repeat it.

## Personalization Strategies That Work

**Segment by**: purchase history, engagement level, demographics, interests, lifecycle stage.
**Use dynamic content**: show different content blocks based on subscriber data.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should small businesses send marketing emails?
1-2 emails per week for most small businesses. Monitor unsubscribe rates — if they spike after increasing frequency, scale back.

### What is a good email open rate for small businesses?
20-25% is good, but varies by industry. E-commerce averages 15-20%, B2B services 25-30%. Focus on improving your own benchmarks.

### Should I buy email lists to grow faster?
Never. Purchased lists violate GDPR and CAN-SPAM, damage sender reputation, and result in poor engagement.

## Conclusion

Implementing these email marketing best practices builds stronger customer relationships and drives consistent revenue. Start with one improvement today — segmenting your list, testing subject lines, or cleaning inactive subscribers.
