# Trend Mapper

The half-life of a TikTok or Instagram Reel trend is often measured in days, not weeks. Brands that react slowly either miss the window entirely or post content that feels visibly stale to their audience. Trend Mapper helps content creators and ecommerce brand teams quickly identify which trending audio tracks, video formats, and meme structures are currently gaining velocity in their product category, and then provides a concrete adaptation blueprint — showing exactly how to slot your product into the format without it feeling forced or off-brand.

## Use when

- You are a TikTok Shop seller in the beauty or skincare category and want to know which trending audio tracks or "get ready with me" video formats are currently breaking through for similar products, so you can brief your creator partners before the trend saturates.
- You manage content for a fitness or wellness brand on Instagram Reels and you have noticed a new visual format or caption meme spreading through your competitors' feeds, and you want to understand its structure and produce your own version within 48 hours before it peaks.
- You are preparing a TikTok Live or short-form content calendar for the next two weeks and want to incorporate 2–3 trend-native content angles alongside your standard product demos to improve organic reach without abandoning your brand voice.
- A competitor's video recently went viral using a sound or format you don't recognize, and you want to analyze the structure of that format, understand why it works for commerce content, and determine whether it is appropriate for your own product category and audience.

## What this skill does

Trend Mapper processes the product category, platform, and audience description you provide and maps the current content landscape across three layers: trending audio (sounds or music gaining shares), viral video formats (structural templates like "POV:", "Things that just make sense", transformation reveals, or duet/stitch formats), and meme or text-overlay templates that are spreading in adjacent niches. For each trend identified, it assesses commercial viability — whether the trend has historically converted well for product content or tends to perform purely as entertainment — and then produces a platform-specific adaptation brief showing how your product should be positioned within the format, what hook line to open with, which product moment to feature, and where to place the call-to-action. Trends are prioritized by estimated remaining virality window so you know which to act on first.

## Inputs required

- **Product category and product name** (required): The specific product and its niche, e.g., "LED face mask, at-home skincare devices" or "protein coffee creamer, functional food and beverage."
- **Target platform** (required): Where you plan to post, e.g., "TikTok," "Instagram Reels," or "YouTube Shorts." Each platform has distinct trend cycles and format conventions.
- **Target audience** (required): Demographic and psychographic description, e.g., "women 22–35 interested in affordable luxury skincare" or "gym-going men 18–30 focused on performance nutrition."
- **Brand voice or content restrictions** (optional): Any tonal guidelines or content types to avoid, e.g., "we do not use humor that references body shaming" or "our brand voice is aspirational and calm, not hype-driven." This prevents trend recommendations that conflict with brand identity.
- **Recent competitor content or trend reference** (optional): A description of a specific video format, sound, or meme you have already noticed spreading, e.g., "I keep seeing videos that start with 'things my dermatologist doesn't want you to know' over a lo-fi beat." Providing this anchors the analysis to your specific observation.

## Output format

The output is organized into three sections. The **Trend Landscape Summary** provides a brief overview of the content environment in your specified category right now, noting the dominant mood or aesthetic (e.g., "educational and trust-building formats are outperforming pure entertainment in skincare right now") and identifying 5–7 specific trends across audio, format, and meme categories with a one-line description of each. The **Prioritized Trend Adaptation Cards** section contains 3–5 detailed cards, each covering: trend name and description, estimated virality window (early / peak / fading), commercial conversion potential (high / medium / low with reasoning), and a step-by-step adaptation blueprint showing the opening hook line, key product moment, and CTA phrasing tailored to your product. The **Content Calendar Placement** section maps the recommended trends to a 2-week posting schedule, indicating which trend to execute first given timing, which pairs well with a product launch or promotion, and which is lower urgency.

## Scope

- Designed for: ecommerce brand teams, content creators, TikTok Shop sellers, social media managers
- Platform context: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, TikTok Shop
- Language: English

## Limitations

- This skill does not have access to real-time trending data from TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube; trend recommendations are based on training knowledge and the reference content you describe, so you should verify current trend velocity directly in the app's Discover or Creative Center before committing to production.
- Trend windows move quickly; adaptation blueprints produced today may be less relevant in 5–7 days if a trend peaks and saturates faster than expected.
- This skill does not produce actual video scripts, voiceovers, or edited content — it produces the strategic adaptation blueprint that informs your production process.
